The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite. Next to the Sun, the Moon exerts the greatest influence on the Earth itself, most notably through its affect on Earth's tides.

MASS AND ORIGIN
The Moon is the largest natural satelite relative to the size of its primary having 27% percent the diameter and 60% the density of the Earth. The Moon is larger than Pluto. The current prevailing hypothesis for the origin of the Moon is that it formed as a result of Mars-sized body colliding with the newly-formed proto-Earth. The material ejected from this impact accreted to form the Moon 4.53 billion years ago.
