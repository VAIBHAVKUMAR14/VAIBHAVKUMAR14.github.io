The Sun, also referred to as "Sol", is the star at the center of the Solar System. Sun's mass accounts for some 99.86% of the total mass of the Solar System.

COMPOSITION AND GALAXY
Roughly three-quarters of the Sun's mass is hydrogen, with the rest mostly helium. Only 1.69% of the Sun (which is still 5 628 times the mass of the Earth) is made up of heavier elements like carbon, iron, neon and oxygen.Once thought to be a relatively insignificant star, the Sun is now considered to be brighter than about 85% of the stars in the Milky Way galaxy. It is located about two thirds of the way out from the centre of the Milky Way.
